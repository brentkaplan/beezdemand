# beezdemand: Behavioral Economic Easy Demand

Facilitates many of the analyses performed in studies of behavioral
economic demand. The package supports commonly-used options for modeling
operant demand including (1) data screening proposed by Stein,
Koffarnus, Snider, Quisenberry, & Bickel (2015;
[doi:10.1037/pha0000020](https://doi.org/10.1037/pha0000020) ), (2)
fitting models of demand such as linear (Hursh, Raslear, Bauman, &
Black, 1989,
[doi:10.1007/978-94-009-2470-3_22](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-94-009-2470-3_22)
), exponential (Hursh & Silberberg, 2008,
[doi:10.1037/0033-295X.115.1.186](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.115.1.186)
) and modified exponential (Koffarnus, Franck, Stein, & Bickel, 2015,
[doi:10.1037/pha0000045](https://doi.org/10.1037/pha0000045) ), and (3)
calculating numerous measures relevant to applied behavioral economists
(Intensity, Pmax, Omax). Also supports plotting and comparing data.
